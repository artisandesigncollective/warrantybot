# WarrantyBot — Pain Points

## Primary Pain Point
> **"My refrigerator/car/phone broke under warranty, but the company says 'we don't cover that' or 'you voided it by opening it.' It's going to cost $2,000 to fix it myself."**

### 1. Illegal "Tie-In" Denials
- **Problem:** Car dealers say "you didn't get your oil changed here, so your engine warranty is void." This is FEDERALLY ILLEGAL under 15 U.S.C. §2302(c).
- **Our Solution:** AI cites the exact code that forces them to honor it unless they can PROVE your oil caused the failure.

### 2. "Warranty Void if Removed" Stickers
- **Problem:** Electronics companies use stickers that are unenforceable in the US.
- **Our Solution:** Demand letter citing FTC warnings to Sony/Microsoft/Nintendo about these illegal stickers.

### 3. Intentional Delay
- **Problem:** Manufacturers keep your device for 4 weeks hoping you'll just buy a new one.
- **Our Solution:** Demand a "loaner" or immediate replacement under the "Reasonable Time" provision of the Act.

## User Personas
- **"The Car Owner"**: Facing a $1k+ repair the dealer refuses to cover.
- **"The Tech Enthusiast"**: Their $2k MacBook screen failed but Apple claims "liquid damage" incorrectly.

## Current Alternatives
1. **Pay for repair** (70%) — Expensive loss.
2. **Buy new** (20%) — What the company wants you to do.
3. **Small Claims**: Slow, but WarrantyBot's demand letter often settles it before court.
